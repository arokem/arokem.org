I am a Research Associate Professor at the University of Washington [Department of Psychology](https://psych.uw.edu/) and a Data Science Fellow at the [eScience Institute](https://escience.washington.edu/).

I am the principal investigator of the [Neuroinformatics R&D Group](https://neuroinformatics.uw.edu/)

I direct the [NeuroHackademy](https://neurohackademy.org/) Summer Institute in Neuroimaging and Data Science.

I am the co-director of the UW [Center for Human Neuroscience](https://depts.washington.edu/chnadmin/).

## Professional biography

Ariel Rokem received a Bachelors and Masters degree in Biology and Cognitive Psychology at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem (2002 and 2005). He then received a PhD in neuroscience from UC Berkeley (2010) and additional postdoctoral training in computational neuroimaging at Stanford (2011 â€“ 2015). He was a Senior Data Scientist at the University of Washington eScience Institute (2015-2020), before joining the faculty of the Department of Psychology in 2020. His group (https://neuroinformatics.uw.edu/) develops computational tools to study the biological basis of brain function and applies them to a variety of research questions. He is a contributor to Python open-source tools for scientific computing, and a member of the Software and Data Carpentry communities, where he has been an instructor since 2013 and an instructor trainer since 2015. He also directs the NIH-funded Summer Institute for Neuroimaging and Data Science (https://neurohackademy.org/).
